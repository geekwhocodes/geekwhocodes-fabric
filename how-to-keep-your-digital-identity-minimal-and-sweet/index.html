    How to keep your digital identity minimal and sweet

    Posted on 09 Feb 2017

    Geekwhocodes
    tweets as @geekwhocodes

    Once, my friend challenged me to find her mobile number without asking any of her friends. Guess what! I accepted that challenge and came up with almost all her details along with her parent's information too and she was like WTF.

    Now, you might be wondering how I got that information. I simply searched her name on Google and other sources. Sometimes most of us unnecessarily feed all our personal data to social networking sites being ignorant about the fact that it can be exposed publicly. Here's how you can keep it minimal.

    Keep your social profiles isolated from search engines

    Social networking sites are the first place to find anyone's information very easily, you need to get rid of it. Popular sites (at least Facebook, LinkedIn, and Quora) gives us the option to block google from indexing your profile. You can find it in privacy settings under profile information. It takes some time to detect the change and de-index the page or you can explicitly send a request to do so.
    There is a site that take snapshots of webpages called "Internet Archive". If you are curious, check if they have any content associated with your profile or alternatively you can check here. If yes, you can send an email to info@archive.org to remove it.
    If you do this, your profile won't come up on search engine's results.

    Change your profile settings

    There are many websites which give you access to configure what other people can see when someone visits or searches your profile. The default settings are always public and make your content publicly available. You can change those privacy settings in such a way that people can see the very minimal information.

    Delete or deactivate some of them

    If you find that the website does not provide options for setting privacy or at least blocking Google or any other search engine from indexing your content, you can deactivate or even delete your account. The problem with deleting an account is that most sites do "Soft Delete". This means the server keeps all your data and just hides it from the people. If you don't like it, you can edit your profile in a way that it remains with almost useless data and then delete it. If the website doesn't version your content, then this will guarantee you that the stored information is useless.

    Use an alias instead of your real name

    You can use an alias like ghost123, bladevil123, ghost00 etc. to go online instead of your real name. It makes sense until and unless they don't know about your alias. It helps at some level but you can find many other ways to do so.

    *If you want to talk about politics, racism, sarcasm or things like that I would recommend creating another account for those purposes. It'll help to keep your professional profile clean.


    Be safe while using online resources ;)

    If you have other suggestions, I would love to hear from you.
